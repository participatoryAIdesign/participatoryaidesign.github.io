Call for Participation

We invite researchers, practitioners, and professionals who work at the intersection of AI, public policy, and civic engagement to participate in our upcoming workshop, Participation, Procurement & Proof of Impact in Public Sector AI Innovation. Comprising two 90-minute sessions, this workshop will explore critical topics and methods that promote public and community participatory engagement in the design, procurement, deployment, and evaluation of public sector AI tools that meet societal needs. We will explore the following themes at this workshop:

1. Evaluating Emerging Participatory Methods

    Participants will discuss and assess the feasibility and opportunities of implementing different participatory design methods and measures, including identifying measurements that document and track public input and outcomes from their efforts. Examining case studies and brainstorming activities, we will assess how hard-to-collect and high-value measures can support participatory design methods that meet societal expectations around equity and privacy issues, legal mandates, and budgetary constraints.

2. Assessing Key Elements that Enhance Participation and Documentable Outcomes.

    This theme focuses on identifying the essential elements that foster effective public participation in the public sector, as well as exploring documentable and/or measurable outcomes that can demonstrate the success of engagement efforts. We will discuss strategies for building trust, ensuring transparency, and capturing diverse perspectives necessary for equitable AI systems.

3. Embedding Participation Requirements in AI Procurement Contracts.

    As federal and local agencies acquire AI tools from the private sector instead of building them in-house, this theme will explore how to institutionalize participant requirements in procurement contracts in feasible, verifiable, and non-burdensome ways for public agencies.
